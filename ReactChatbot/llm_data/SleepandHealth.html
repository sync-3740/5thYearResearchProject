SLEEP AND YOUR HEALTH

(0 Reviews)

You’ve probably noticed that a night of poor sleep can have a serious impact on your mood and energy levels the next day. The good news is that a single night doesn’t affect your overall health. The bad news, though, is that consistently getting less sleep can increase your risk of high blood pressure, heart disease, and even gaining weight.

When you are tired you might be more likely to reach for sugary snacks throughout the day and to skip exercise. Lack of sleep can also affect the hormones in your body that make you hungry and keep you feeling full.

Aim for 6-8 hours of sleep each night to maintain a healthy lifestyle and heart. It has even been found that having a couple of naps each week could decrease your risk of heart attacks and strokes.
