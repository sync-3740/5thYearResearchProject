WHAT CAUSES HYPERTENSION?

(0 Reviews)

Some diseases, such as kidney disease and diabetes, can lead to higher blood pressure. This is known as secondary hypertension. Most of the people with high blood pressure, however, have primary or essential hypertension. This means their high blood pressure is not caused by an underlying disease.

We do not know exactly what causes high blood pressure in primary hypertension. In most cases, there may not be one single cause, but rather several small causes which together cause a substantial risk of hypertension. These are known as risk factors, of which there are two kinds: modifiable and non-modifiable.

Modifiable risk factors are things you can change. By making changes to your lifestyle, you can significantly lower your blood pressure. Some of these changes include:

  • Decreasing the amount of salt and fat in your diet
  • Doing regular physical exercise
  • Losing weight
  • Stopping smoking
  • Decreasing your alcohol consumption.

 

You can find more information about healthy lifestyle choices in this app.

Non-modifiable risk factors are things you cannot change and include sex, age, and ethnicity. In general, men have higher blood pressure than women. After menopause, however, women tend to have higher blood pressure. Blood pressure increases along with age and older people tend to have higher blood pressure, irrespective of sex.

Some people also carry genes that make them more likely to develop high blood pressure. We don’t yet know what all of these genes are or how they work, but if you have a family history of hypertension or other heart conditions, you could be at higher risk too. Hypertension also seems to be more prevalent in Black African, African Caribbean, and South Asian populations.
