HOW DO I KNOW HOW MUCH SALT IS IN MY FOOD?

(0 Reviews)

Your intake of salt is not just how much table salt you add to your food once it is made. There is lots of salt already in the food we buy in the supermarkets, from a loaf of bread to ready meals. In fact, 75% of the salt we eat has been added to our food before we buy it.

A good way to reduce the amount of salt in your diet is to check the nutritional labels on the food you buy in the supermarket. These food labels will show the amount of salt (or sodium) and often use a traffic light system to show if the food has a low, medium, or high salt content. Eating food low in salt (green) and reducing the amount of food you eat with medium (amber) and high (red) levels of salt, will help to reduce your blood pressure and reduce the risk of developing high blood pressure.

It is still important to control how much salt you add to food when cooking, remembering that a teaspoon of salt is your entire recommended daily intake. Using alternative flavours such as lemon juice, black pepper, and herbs are a good way to cut down on the extra salt you add to your food while keeping your food nice and tasty.

Remember that sauces and condiments you might regularly use to flavour food can also have a surprising amount of salt. Condiments such as ketchup, mustard, brown sauce, and soy sauce can have a very high amount of salt in them. Making a switch to the reduced-salt options available in the supermarkets, or herbs and spices will help reduce the amount of salt you eat.

As well as checking the salt content on food labels and adding less salt while cooking, there are other small changes that can make a difference. Swapping ready-made meals and processed snacks for healthier, home-made options are also a great way to reduce the amount of salt in your diet. Below are some examples of simple swaps:
