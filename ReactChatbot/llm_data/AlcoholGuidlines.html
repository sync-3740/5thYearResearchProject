RECOMMENDED GUIDELINES FOR DRINKING ALCOHOL

(0 Reviews)

There is no “healthy” level of drinking, but it is recommended that you drink no more than 14 units of alcohol per week. Try to keep several alcohol-free days a week and avoid binge drinking. If you are going to drink all 14 units in one week, space them over three or four days.

Drinking a large amount of alcohol in one sitting (more than 6 units for women or 8 units for men) amounts to binge drinking. That’s the equivalent of three or four pints in one go. Binge drinking can have serious effects on your health, because of the stress it puts on your body. You are at a higher risk of injuring yourself from falls, accidents, and alcohol poisoning. You could also hurt those around you if drinking changes your behaviour. When it comes to your blood pressure, frequent binge drinking can cause long term increases in your blood pressure.

How Much Is a “Unit” of Alcohol?

Alcohol units aren’t simply counted in the number of drinks you have. Drinks can be different sizes and different strengths, so a unit is based on both the amount and the strength of what you drink. Alcohol strength is measures as “alcohol by volume” (ABV), which is a percentage of alcohol in your whole drink. Units simplify measuring your alcohol consumption into one drink.

The ABV varies widely between drinks, and so do the units between drinks. In the UK all cans and bottles should tell you how many units are in it.
